Kingston on the Edge (KOTE) is a ten-day multi-venue studio and performing summer arts festival that began in 2007.  It’s the brainchild of five friends who wanted to showcase Jamaican artists in a creative and free manner. KOTE is a celebration of the innovative art and diverse artists who live, work and/or are inspired by Kingston.

 

The purpose of the Festival is to promote the Arts in Kingston as something that everyone, irrespective of background can appreciate and enjoy.  From its inception, the idea has been to promote positive creative energy throughout our city and the majority of the events are free and open to the public. Once established, it quickly became a much anticipated fixture on the local art calendar.  KOTE is the largest festival of its kind in the English speaking Caribbean.

 

More than 450 artists and performers, from as afar as The Republic of Georgia, The United States, France, Spain, Venezuela, Australia, Poland, Canada, Haiti, Cuba and Brazil, have participated.

 

Beginning around the summer solstice, the capital's parks, galleries, restaurants, bars, theatres and even the city's synagogue have been among the dozens of venues that showcase the creative spirit of Kingston.  The Festival features painting, sculpture, photography, film, dance, theatre, music, performance art, installations and anything else one can think of in the realm of artistic expression.

 

KOTE provides a forum outside of traditional spaces that allows artists to express themselves and push the boundaries and definitions of their art.  We have found that this environment motivates and inspires the artist to experiment with their work, often leading to the birth of new and exciting movements in the local scene.

 

There is no other festival in Jamaica which does what KOTE does.

 

The Festival offers an opportunity for artists, venues and the general public to interact creatively, with a view to developing and harnessing the incredible artistic potential of Jamaica.
