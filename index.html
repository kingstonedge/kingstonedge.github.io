It’s a Buddhist truth that this moment contains all others.  It’s a human truth that we’re all in this together.  Now, in other words, is the time.

 

Kingston On The Edge (KOTE) Urban Arts Festival, formerly the largest such event in the English speaking Caribbean, is returning, after a three year hiatus.  The Festival’s success was always based on people making it their own, and we’re very excited to have our existing family enlarged to include the young and fresh new voices currently making themselves heard.

 

The intersection of peoples, places and ideas have always been central to Kingston’s identity, and to KOTE’s.  From the waterfront to the mountains, uptown to downtown, fashionable to down-market, the festival has sought to use art and creativity to address and balance the negativity and separation so often associated with our city.  Art is, above all, open; and KOTE’s modus operandi has always been multidisciplinary and interactionist- incorporating the visual, performing and other disciplines into a particularly Jamaican and urban mélange, all the while breaking down the barriers that keep us apart.

 

We’re happy to be back, and can’t wait to see you all again for the first time!
